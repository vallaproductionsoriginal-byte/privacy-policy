User Rights and Data Deletion Requests

Harmony Engine Plus does not require users to create an account and does not maintain user profiles or a database containing names, email addresses, musical projects, MIDI performances, or other user-generated content.

Users may nevertheless request the deletion of technical information that may be present in licence-verification, security, or server logs.

How to Submit a Data Deletion Request

To request deletion of data associated with the use of Harmony Engine Plus, send an email to:

vallaproductionsoriginal@gmail.com

Use the following subject line:

Harmony Engine Plus - Data Deletion Request

The request should include, where available:

  • the approximate date and time when the app was used or the verification request occurred;
  • the application version installed at that time;
  • the country or time zone from which the request was made;
  • the public IP address used at that time, if known;
  • a brief description of the data or event concerned.

Users should not send passwords, Google account credentials, payment details, copies of identity documents, device serial numbers, advertising identifiers, or other unnecessary sensitive information.

Processing of the Request

Valla Productions will review the information provided and make reasonable efforts to identify any relevant technical records.

If the records can be reasonably identified, the associated technical data will be deleted or anonymised unless its continued retention is necessary for:

  • compliance with a legal obligation;
  • the establishment, exercise, or defence of legal claims;
  • the investigation of fraud, abuse, piracy, or a security incident;
  • the protection of the app, verification server, developer, or users.

Deletion requests will normally be processed within 30 days. Where permitted by applicable law, this period may be extended if the request is unusually complex or if multiple requests have been submitted. In such cases, the user will be informed by email.

The developer may request limited additional technical information when reasonably necessary to locate the relevant records. The developer will not request a password, Google account credentials, or payment information.

Data That May Be Deleted

Depending on what is available and identifiable, a deletion request may cover:

  • IP addresses contained in server access or security logs;
  • licence-verification timestamps;
  • request identifiers, hashes, nonces, or verification references;
  • application version and package information associated with a request;
  • integrity or licensing verdicts stored in technical logs;
  • error, rate-limit, and anti-abuse records associated with the request.

Data Stored Locally on the Device

A valid licence-verification result may be stored locally on the user's device for up to seven days.

Users may remove locally stored app data by using the Android system settings:

  1. open Android Settings;
  2. select Apps;
  3. select Harmony Engine Plus;
  4. select Storage;
  5. select Clear data or Clear storage.

Uninstalling Harmony Engine Plus also removes locally stored application data from the device, subject to Android and Google backup settings.

Limitations

Because Harmony Engine Plus does not create user accounts and does not assign a permanent developer-controlled identifier to each user, it may not always be possible to associate an individual person with a specific technical log entry.

If the relevant records cannot be identified using the information provided, Valla Productions will explain this in its response. Technical logs are otherwise deleted or anonymised according to the retention periods described in this Privacy Policy.

Complaints and Additional Rights

Depending on applicable law, users may also have the right to request access, correction, restriction, or objection to the processing of their personal data.

Users located in the European Economic Area may lodge a complaint with their competent data-protection authority if they believe that their personal data has been processed unlawfully.
